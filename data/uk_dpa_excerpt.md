# UK Data Protection Act 2018 — Selected Sections (condensed excerpt)

A condensed, illustrative excerpt of the UK Data Protection Act 2018, used as a
second example domain for agentic-reg. Each "## Section N" becomes one chunk and
one node in the knowledge graph; cross-references ("Section 3") become edges.
This demonstrates that the framework is not GDPR-shaped: the unit is "section",
not "article", and the citation scheme is `[section-N]`.

## Section 1 — Overview

This Act makes provision about the processing of personal data. Most processing
is governed by the data protection principles and the rights of data subjects.
Terms used throughout the Act are defined in Section 3, and the general
conditions for lawful processing are set out in Section 8.

## Section 2 — Protection of personal data

A controller must ensure that personal data is processed in accordance with the
data protection principles. Processing that does not comply with those
principles, or that lacks a lawful basis under Section 8, is unlawful. The
principles themselves are set out in Section 35, and the meaning of key terms is
given in Section 3.

## Section 3 — Terms relating to the processing of personal data

In this Act: 'personal data' means information relating to an identified or
identifiable living individual; 'processing' means an operation performed on
personal data such as collection, storage, use, or disclosure; 'controller'
means the person who determines the purposes and means of processing; and 'data
subject' means the individual to whom personal data relates. These terms apply
wherever they are used, including in Section 8 and Section 35.

## Section 8 — Lawfulness of processing

The processing of personal data is lawful only if and to the extent that it
meets at least one lawful basis: the data subject has given consent; processing
is necessary for the performance of a contract; processing is necessary for
compliance with a legal obligation; processing is necessary to protect vital
interests; or processing is necessary for the performance of a task in the
public interest. Processing must also comply with the principles in Section 35,
and special categories of data are subject to the additional conditions in
Section 10.

## Section 10 — Special categories of personal data

Processing of special categories of personal data — such as data revealing
health, racial or ethnic origin, or biometric data used to identify a person —
is permitted only where, in addition to a lawful basis under Section 8, a
specific condition is met. Those conditions include the explicit consent of the
data subject and processing necessary for reasons of substantial public
interest. These requirements supplement, and do not replace, the general
lawfulness rule in Section 8.

## Section 35 — The data protection principles

Personal data must be processed lawfully, fairly and transparently; collected
for specified, explicit and legitimate purposes; adequate, relevant and limited
to what is necessary; accurate and kept up to date; kept no longer than
necessary; and processed securely. A controller is responsible for, and must be
able to demonstrate, compliance with these principles. The requirement to
process securely is given practical effect by the security duty in Section 66.

## Section 45 — Right of access

A data subject is entitled to obtain from the controller confirmation as to
whether their personal data is being processed and, where it is, access to that
data together with information about the purposes of the processing and the
recipients of the data. The terms used in this right take their meaning from
Section 3. Access may be restricted only to the extent necessary and
proportionate.

## Section 47 — Rights to rectification and erasure

A data subject has the right to obtain the rectification of inaccurate personal
data without undue delay, and the erasure of personal data where its processing
would infringe the data protection principles or where retention is no longer
necessary. These rights build on the right of access in Section 45 and are
assessed against the principles in Section 35.

## Section 66 — Security of processing

The controller and any processor must implement appropriate technical and
organisational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risks
of the processing, including measures to protect against unauthorised access,
loss, or destruction of personal data. This duty gives effect to the security
requirement within the principles in Section 35 and underpins the breach
obligations in Section 67.

## Section 67 — Notification of a personal data breach

Where a personal data breach occurs, the controller must notify the Commissioner
without undue delay and, where feasible, within 72 hours of becoming aware of it,
unless the breach is unlikely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of
individuals. The notification must describe the breach, its likely consequences,
and the measures taken. The adequacy of those measures is judged against the
security duty in Section 66.
